Frequently Asked Questions

Common signs of a gas leak include a rotten egg or sulfur smell, a hissing or whistling sound near a gas line, visible damage to the gas line, dead or discolored vegetation around the pipeline, or feeling lightheaded or dizzy.

If you suspect a gas leak, immediately evacuate the area, avoid using electrical devices or open flames, and call your gas company or emergency services from a safe location.

Gas lines should be inspected at least once a year to ensure they are in good condition and to prevent potential leaks or hazards. It is also recommended to have inspections when moving into a new property or after any significant ground movement.

The property owner is typically responsible for the maintenance and repair of gas lines that run from the meter throughout the property. The gas company usually maintains the gas meter and any lines leading up to it.

Look for a service provider that is licensed and insured, has positive customer reviews, offers emergency services, and is experienced with both residential and commercial gas line systems.
