Frequently Asked Questions

To install a dishwasher, you will typically need a screwdriver, an adjustable wrench, pliers, a level, a tape measure, a drill, and sometimes a hole saw if modifications to cabinetry are necessary.

Ensure there is a plumbing and electrical connection point in the cabinet space where the dishwasher will be installed. Verify that there is sufficient space for the dimensions of the dishwasher, and check that the floor is level.

If you have experience with plumbing and electrical work, you may be able to install a dishwasher yourself. However, if you're unsure or uncomfortable with the process, it is recommended to hire a professional to ensure proper installation and compliance with local codes.

First, turn off the water supply. Connect the dishwasher water inlet valve to the hot water supply line using a flexible water supply tube and a compression fitting. Ensure that all connections are tight to prevent leaks.

If your dishwasher doesn't fit, measure the space again to confirm the dimensions. You may need to adjust the cabinetry or choose a different model that better fits the space. Ensure that appliance installation follows the manufacturer's guidelines and complies with local building codes.
